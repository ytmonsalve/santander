Interesting Facts

Did you know the Chicamocha Canyon is the second largest worldwide?

With a maximum depth of 6,600 ft, an area of 270,000 acres, and a length of 141 mi. It is a major tourist attraction approximately 34 mi from the capital of Santander.

Did you know Bucaramanga is the capital of Santander?

Bucaramanga has the fifth-largest economy in Colombia, and is the ninth most populous city in the country. Bucaramanga has been given the nickname of "The City of Parks" since it has over 160 parks throughout the city.

Did you know Colombia exports approximately 12.4 million 60-kg bags of coffee a year?

Colombia has a perfect combination of soil, weather, and hand-picked coffee beans that makes their coffee have a unique flavor. The region of Santander has a famous coffee farm in Mesa de Los Santos called Hacienda el Roble. You can taste some of the 72 coffee varieties, and enjoy a-bird friendly coffee safari with over 170 bird species.

Did you know Santander was named after Francisco de Paula Santander?

Soldier and statesman Francisco de Paula Santander fought beside Simon Bolivar in the war for South American independence. He served as presiedent of Colombia from 1833 to 1837. Santander was an able statesman, he was known for his democratic republican principles and efficient procedures.
